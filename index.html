My Top 5 Favorite Video Games!

By Richard Hsu

1. Persona 5


Photo by Steam .

P5 was a defining part of my high school year. It gave me the confidence to stand up to injustice as well as gave me a story that I have fond memories of. Following a protaganist who was banished to Tokyo for a crime he diddn't commit, he seeks to gather a band of Phantom Thieves to change the hearts of corrupt adults. From the gameplay, story, music, and UI, Persona 5 is a standout hit that got me into Japanese turn-based RPGs. A massive recommend from me.

2. Limbus Company


Photo by Steam .

I came into this live-service game with no expectations and was greatly surprised. Limbus Company is a Korean turn-based live service RPG. As the Executive Manager of Limbus Company, you lead your group of twelve Sinners based off of classic literature. It's an immensely emotional and dramatic story with amazing art and music.

3. NieR: Automata


Photo by Steam.

NieR: Automata is WEIRD. But in a good way! A JRPG with an emphasis on action with a diverse combat system, the story is about searching for meaning in a meaningless world, as the Androids fight off the Machines in service of Humanity. With three different routes of the game, there's plenty of strangeness to encounter here.

4. Fate/Grand Order


Photo by MMOCulture .

FGO is the longest live-service game I've played. Written by a legendary Japanese writer, it is well known for the richness of its story that uses a variety of anime interpretations of historical figures to do battle. Due to its gacha mechanics, I struggle to recommend it to others, but it's a great game!

5. Hades


Photo by Steam .

An action-based roguelike, Hades is a reverse-Orpheus story about Zagreus, a god escaping to the surface while Hades, his father tries to prevent him from doing so. It seems hard at first, and you are expected to die a lot. However, you eventually learn the enemy attack patterns after fighting and dying to them over and over. It's a really great game if you have a lot of time to spend.
